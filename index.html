Optimizing Small Nuclear Power Plant Placement in Brazil

A 4D proposal for efficient placement and operation of Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) based on comprehensive demographic and geographic analysis.

Explore Maps

The Challenge

Brazil's diverse geography and uneven population distribution present unique challenges for energy distribution. Our project develops a multidimensional approach (4D: 3 spatial dimensions + time) to identify optimal locations for Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) considering demographic trends, energy demand projections, and geographic constraints.

4D Methodology

1. Data Collection & Processing

Electricity generation data were collected over a continuous multi-year period for each Brazilian state, allowing the construction of time series that revealed the states with the highest energy output and highlighted regional growth trends in power generation.

In addition, state-level electricity consumption data were gathered to compare current consumption with generation capacity and to estimate future energy demand.

To support this analysis, data from IBGE, EPE, and ANEEL were integrated to develop comprehensive, multi-dimensional datasets. These datasets provide a robust foundation for identifying the most suitable regions for the deployment of Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) in Brazil.

3. Optimization Model

We developed a proprietary algorithm that considers:

  • Minimum viable population within 50km radius
  • Proximity to existing transmission infrastructure
  • Water resource availability and quality
  • Projected regional development trajectories
  • Security and emergency response considerations
